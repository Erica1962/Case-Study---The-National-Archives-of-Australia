The National Archives of Australia

Responsive Design Website

Project Overview

This case study investigated the usability of the National Archives of Australia website from the perspective of a immigrant searching for the arrival date in Australia. A prototype of the homepage was created to address the issues identified to increase navability and readability.

See Prototype

Project Summary

The National Archives of Australia (NAA) is the home of Australia's documented history of important events. Offering online records searches for a wide variety of catalogues, workshops, exhibitions and events the NAA provides the public a place to learn and remember Australia's past.

The intent of this project was to increase visibility of the online records and catalogues offerings of the NAA to heighten awareness of the wealth of knowledge freely searchable for students, researchers and the general public.

The Problem

The National Archives of Australia (NAA) is the primary location for people who immigrated to Australia before 1973 to find their immigration date. This information is critical for filling in forms for some Government agencies. However, the current design doesn't highlight that the records are searchable online.

The Solution

Redesigning the NAA website’s primary and secondary navigation, and highlighting that the records are searchable online, would provide the user with clear direction on how to find their imigration date in the records search.

Understanding the User
To set the scene of the potential user of the National Archives of Australia website and what they might be experiencing as problems using the website, a Proto Persona was created to identify what the demographics of the website’s potential user might be. This was followed by an assessment of the current website to establish the most likely user flows taken by the user in navigating their way to the immigration section of the website, a huristics assessment and WCAG colour accessibility testing.
User Interface Analysis
Building on the initial ideas of the potential user and how they might perceive the website a user research plan was created and implemented to conduct an analysis of the interface from the perspective of a real use. Aspects for investigation included navigation of the website, heuristics and any pain points the user identifies.
User Test Plan
Objectives:
  • To test how intuitive it is to identify and progress through the immigration search of the NAA website.
  • To test if the user is able to identify how to purchase a digital copy without signing up to NAA.
  • Identify pain points the user encounters throughout the searching process.
